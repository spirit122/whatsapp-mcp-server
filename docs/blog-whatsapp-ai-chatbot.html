Build a WhatsApp AI Chatbot with Auto-Reply in 2026

Customers expect instant responses on WhatsApp. With the WhatsApp MCP Server's auto-reply feature, you can deploy an AI chatbot that answers customer questions 24/7 using your choice of AI provider — OpenAI, Claude, Gemini, or Groq. No coding required. This tutorial covers everything from setup to customizing system prompts for your specific business.

How Auto-Reply Works

The WhatsApp MCP Server includes a built-in auto-reply module that intercepts incoming WhatsApp messages via webhooks and generates AI-powered responses automatically. Here's the flow:

  1. A customer sends a WhatsApp message to your business number
  2. The webhook receives the message on Cloudflare Workers (under 50ms)
  3. The server sends the message to your configured AI provider with your system prompt
  4. The AI generates a response based on your business context
  5. The response is sent back to the customer on WhatsApp instantly

All of this happens in 1-3 seconds, giving your customers a near-instant reply experience.

Supported AI Providers

You can connect any of these AI providers to power your auto-reply chatbot:

OpenAI

GPT-4o, GPT-4o-mini. Best for general-purpose conversations and complex reasoning.

Claude (Anthropic)

Gemini 2.0 Flash. Fast responses with strong multilingual support.

Groq

LLaMA 3, Mixtral. Ultra-fast inference for high-volume messaging.

Step 1: Enable Webhooks

Auto-reply requires the Pro plan or higher, since it depends on webhooks to receive incoming messages. After upgrading, enable webhooks in the dashboard.

Configure your webhook URL in the Meta Developer Portal to point to:

https://whatsapp-mcp-server.eosspirit.workers.dev/webhook

Step 2: Configure Your AI Provider

In the MCP Server dashboard, navigate to the Auto-Reply section. Select your AI provider and enter your API key.

// Example dashboard configuration
{
  "autoReply": {
    "enabled": true,
    "provider": "openai",
    "model": "gpt-4o-mini",
    "apiKey": "sk-...",
    "systemPrompt": "You are a helpful assistant for...",
    "maxTokens": 500
  }
}
Cost tip: For high-volume messaging, use GPT-4o-mini or Groq's LLaMA models. They provide good quality responses at a fraction of the cost of larger models.

Step 3: Write Your System Prompt

The system prompt defines your chatbot's personality and knowledge. A well-crafted prompt is the difference between a helpful assistant and a frustrating experience. Below are battle-tested examples for different business types.

Restaurant / Food Business

You are the virtual assistant for La Cocina Bistro.

Hours: Mon-Sat 12pm-10pm, closed Sunday.
Address: 456 Main Street, Suite 2.

Menu highlights: grilled salmon ($18), pasta carbonara ($14),
Caesar salad ($10). Daily specials change — ask staff.

Reservations: accept for parties of 4+, same-day only.
Delivery: available via UberEats and DoorDash.

Rules:
- Be friendly and concise (max 2-3 sentences)
- If asked about allergens, recommend calling the restaurant
- For complaints, apologize and offer to connect with manager
- Never invent menu items or prices

E-Commerce Store

You are the support assistant for TechGear Shop.

Shipping: free over $50, standard 3-5 days, express 1-2 days.
Returns: 30-day window, item must be unused, free return label.
Payment: Visa, Mastercard, PayPal, Apple Pay.

Common questions:
- Order tracking: ask for order number, provide tracking link
- Size guide: direct to website.com/size-guide
- Warranty: 1 year on electronics, 6 months on accessories

Rules:
- Always ask for the order number when relevant
- Escalate billing disputes to human support
- Never process refunds — direct to support@techgear.com
- Keep responses under 3 sentences when possible

Customer Support / SaaS

You are the support bot for CloudSync, a file backup SaaS.

Plans: Free (5GB), Pro ($9/mo, 100GB), Business ($29/mo, 1TB).
Status page: status.cloudsync.io

Common issues:
- Sync stuck: ask them to restart the desktop app
- Storage full: recommend upgrading or clearing deleted files
- Login issues: direct to cloudsync.io/reset-password
- Billing: cannot modify — direct to billing@cloudsync.io

Rules:
- Be technical but accessible
- If the issue needs investigation, collect their email
  and account ID, then escalate
- Never share internal system details
- Respond in the same language the customer writes in

Step 4: Test Your Chatbot

Send a test message to your WhatsApp Business number from a personal phone. You should receive an AI-generated response within 1-3 seconds. Try different scenarios:

  • Ask a question that's covered in your system prompt
  • Ask something outside scope to verify the bot handles edge cases
  • Send a message in a different language to test multilingual behavior
  • Send an image or voice note to test non-text message handling

Advanced Configuration

Conversation Memory

The auto-reply module maintains a short conversation history per user, so the AI can reference previous messages in the same session. The default window is 10 messages or 30 minutes — whichever comes first.

Keyword-Based Escalation

You can configure keywords that automatically escalate the conversation to a human agent. Common trigger words include "speak to agent", "human", "manager", or "complaint". When triggered, the bot sends a handoff message and stops auto-replying for that conversation.

Business Hours Filtering

Configure auto-reply to only respond outside business hours, so your human team handles messages during the day while AI covers nights and weekends.

Monitoring and Analytics

Track your chatbot's performance through the dashboard with metrics like response time, messages handled per day, escalation rate, and customer satisfaction. The Pro plan includes full analytics to help you refine your system prompt over time.
